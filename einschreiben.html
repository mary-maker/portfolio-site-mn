Optimizing “Einschreiben”-Landingpage

Deutsche Post

About

This pilot project tackles one of Deutsche Post’s most famous products. In theory a transparent and comprehensible product information of “Einschreiben” will lead to to measurable customer satisfaction. This effect shall additionally increase the revenue on Einschreiben items.

Time and approach

The design process took place within one week for the Google design sprint and additional two weeks for further designing and developing.
The following A/B/N test and customer survey were scheduled to run for four weeks until a significant winner option becomes apparent.

After the sprint

The following iteration was mainly driven by my concepting and wireframing tasks to identify which pattern suits our original idea best. 
And to identify which pattern is already existing in the content management system.

The finding

Our intention to advise an “Einschreiben” option based on the customer’s actual use case worked perfectly. The usability test indicates that this is an appropriate way to communicate with our customers.

Cards and tables

We were already aware of the need to develop the cards pattern in case it was turning out to be the superior option to the control. Keeping in mind that the key finding of the Design Sprint was to explain the product options via use cases, we planned to go with an additional (easier to implement) solution.
As a result we added another option: the use cases translated into the control’s chart visualization.

No mobile

Because the cards were a new pattern on deutschepost.de we were unsure about user’s interaction with them. An additional usability test would have been the right method to get an impression about this. 
Considering our timing and the pilot phase we decided to A/B/N test only for Desktop.

CMS and Target

The external developer and I worked closely together for the implementations.
After finalizing the designs I built the template for the options B and C in the content management system which is only accessable via intranet. In doing so the external coder was able to style the cards independently from the intranet and pasted the custom code directly into Adobe Target.

Preparing the content in AEM for an easier custom styling with CSS.

Winner or not?

The A/B/N Test proved that both options showed an uplift in CTR (Click-through-rate) into the shop. 
We decided to run the test two additional weeks for more significance in CTR, CVR (conversion rate) and RPV (revenue per visit).
